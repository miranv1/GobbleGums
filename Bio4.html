 KALACHUCHI
 Plumeria rubra is a deciduous species of plants within the Plumeria genus. It is a dogbane member of the family that expands
 as a propagation shrub or small tree to something like a tallness of 2–8 m (5–25 ft) and a comparable circumference. It has a
 thick succulent trunk as well as brusque branches encased inside a narrow gray bark. The branches seem to be fragile and,
 once cracked, leak a white sap which can irritate the skin and such mucous membranes. This latex obtained from plants stems
 is hazardous, however not lethal though if found in excess amounts. They are deciduous, with leaves collapsing during the
 cold months of each year. The flowers are short lived and occur at the tips of the branches throughout the warmer months.
 They can grow in a wide range of soil conditions, from acidic to alkaline, fine sand to clay. In tropical places, such plants
 thrive in dry to moderate moisture, well-drained soil structure with direct sunlight and therefore will blossom for the majority
 of the year. They don't really develop well enough in wet soils, so if temperature drops below 50 ℉ throughout the winter, the
 crops would then cease flourishing and lose their leaves. Defined plants often are extremely salt tolerant, withstanding even
 salt-laden breezes.
