A G L A O N E M A   W I D U R I

 CHINESE EVERGREEN
 Aglaonema is a genus of flowering plants in the arum family Araceae. These plants are native to Asia's tropical and subtropical
 areas, as well as New Guinea. Aglaonema is commonly referred to as Chinese evergreens. Cultivars were selected based on
 their shape and structure, as well as the color and sequence of their leaves. There are plenty of stems that are white or cream
 in color. Many were also developed to survive colder temperatures. This aglaonema takes very little attention. Due to its
 greater moisture requirements, aglaonema has been identified by a few gardeners as a potential greenhouse crop. They
 flourish in the warm, smoggy, and vivid environment of a greenhouse, and they're frequently cultivated successfully
 indoors by as closely matching these requirements as possible. The most important thing to remember with aglaonema
 is to keep it wet and warm. If you follow these methods, you will be rewarded with a long-lasting, safe houseplant
 that will not exceed its pot.
