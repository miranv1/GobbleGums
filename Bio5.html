 AROID PLANT
 Alocasia Polly is a well-known plant. Its huge, strangely-shaped leaves with vibrant nerves that will catch your eye from afar.
 On long petioles, the huge, cordate leaves tend to grow to a size of 20-90 cm (8-35 inches). Its araceous flowers appear just at
 the end of a short stalk and are not noticeable; they are frequently concealed behind the leaves. Because alocasias are tropical
 plants, they require a lot of moisture. Often these, which include Alocasia Polly, have tough outer stems that they utilize to
 hold moisture. Alocasia feeds itself by using the moisture preserved in their stems, eliminating the necessity of soil moisture.
 It is highly susceptible to overwatering - it prefers dry soil. They usually die in cold climates or even perhaps the dehydration
 of artificial heating, and yet attempting to gradually acclimate plants indoors could indeed be of service. Once inside, the
 watering span should be limited, as well as the plants must always be kept safe from predatory mites or red spiders.
