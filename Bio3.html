 MONEY TREE
 Pachira aquatica is a tropical swamp tree in the Malvaceae family. It has the potential to reach a height of 18 meters (59.1 ft).
 This plant has smooth green bark as well as shiny green palmate leaves together with lanceolate leaflets. The above species
 tends to form a mildly thicker root with relatively small roots that also functions like a water supply. Its own fancy
 flowers, which are one of the world's biggest tree flowers, have lengthy, slender petals which display much like banana
 peelings that unveil hair - like structures, yellowish-orange stamens. Through moist, frost-free locations, the tree can
 grow well together in a tropical ornamental and could be launched from seed or cutting. This is a fast growing tree that
 reacts well to a variety of environments. It necessitates bright lighting - yet not direct sunlight. The best possible
 performance and life span in crop production could be obtained by keeping it in relatively dry soil, particularly
 during the colder months. It is advisable to avoid a dry indoor environment. It is better sprinkled on a daily basis;
 rather, it will shed all of its leaves. A plant must not be artificially inseminated during its first year as a potted
 plant. Following that, a few fertilizer can be applied throughout summer watering. This plant grows quite quickly in
 favorable circumstances.
