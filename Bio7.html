D I C H O T O M O U S   K E Y
 A dichotomous key is a method of identification whereby groups of organisms are divided into two categories repeatedly. The number of things being considered is reduced at each successive
 step in the key so that by a process of elimination, the correct determination is made. It is usually represented in one of two ways: diagrammatic & descriptive representation. The dichotomous
 key of the 5 given plants, in both representation, is shown below.
 
  1.  
    1. Prefers dry soil? ….. go to 2
    2. Does not prefer dry soil? ….. go to 4

  2.  
    1. The plant has soft stems? ….. go to 3
    2. The plant does not have soft stem? …… Plumeria rubra

  3.  
    1. Prefers moist and warm environment? ….. Alocasia polly
    2. Does not prefer moist and warm environment? ….. Pachira aquatica

  4.  
    1. The plant has white stems? ….. Aglaonema widuri
    2. The plant does not have white stems? ….. Zamioculcas zamifolia
